### The Prior Authorization Trap

Before a doctor can perform many procedures — an MRI, a surgery, a new medication — they need **permission from the patient's insurance company**. This is called a **Prior Authorization (PA)**.

Sounds simple. It is not.

Here's what actually happens:

1. A patient comes in needing an MRI.
2. The doctor's staff logs into the insurance company's website — each insurer has its own portal, its own login, its own confusing layout.
3. They fill out a form — manually — with patient details, diagnosis codes, clinical notes.
4. They wait. Sometimes hours. Sometimes days.
5. The insurance company denies it. No reason given, or a vague one.
6. Staff spends more hours writing an appeal, navigating the portal again, re-uploading documents.
7. Meanwhile, the patient is waiting. The doctor's revenue is on hold.

**This process happens hundreds of times a week — for every clinic.**

### What ClearClaim Actually Does

**1. Eligibility Verification**
When a patient books an appointment, ClearClaim's agent logs into their insurance company's website, checks if they're covered, what their deductible is, and whether the planned treatment needs pre-authorization. Your staff gets a clean summary before the patient even walks in the door.

**2. Prior Authorization Submission**
Your staff tells ClearClaim what procedure is needed. The agent navigates to the correct insurance portal, fills in all the required clinical and patient details, uploads any necessary documents, and submits the request — in minutes, not hours.

**3. Claim Status Tracking**
ClearClaim's agent periodically checks the status of every submitted claim. If a claim is sitting untouched, it flags it. If it's approved, it notifies you. No more logging in just to check a status.

**4. Denial Management & Appeals**
When a claim is denied (and they often are), ClearClaim reads the denial reason, helps prepare an appeal, and resubmits — navigating the portal's appeal process step by step.
